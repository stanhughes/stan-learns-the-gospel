Notes from July 11, 2021

Welcome Message , by President Russell M. Nelson

This is a very short talk. The following points caught my attention.

We gather as a great global family desiring to worship our Lord and Savior, Jesus Christ.

The desire is the why of what we are doing and is very important. If the desire is wrong then the activity cannot be blessed by God. It is difficult to determine someone desire; However, it is clear to God, Who grants unto us according to the righteous desires of our hearts, see Alma 41:3, 5-6

Some lessons that I knew before have been written on my heart in new and instructive ways.

This makes me think, What lessons I need to have written on my heart in new and instructive ways?

...the strength of the Church lies in the efforts and ever-growing testimonies of its members. Testimonies are best cultivated in the home...many of you have dramatically increased the study of the gospel in your homes.

Is my testimony ever-growing? Are we doing things in our home that best [cultivate] testimonies? How can we dramatically [increase] the study of the gospel in [our home]?

I have thought about the need for each of us to remove, with the Savior’s help, the old debris in our lives.

What old debris in [my live] do I need remove, with the Savior's help?

The gospel of Jesus Christ is a gospel of repentance. Because of the Savior’s Atonement, His gospel provides an invitation to keep changing, growing, and becoming more pure. It is a gospel of hope, of healing, and of progress. Thus, the gospel is a message of joy! Our spirits rejoice with every small step forward we take.

Am I changing, growing, and becoming more pure?

...Heavenly Father and His Beloved Son know and love you individually. They stand ready to assist you in every step forward you take.

Heavenly Father and His Beloved Son know and love [me]. They stand ready to assist [me] in every step forward [I] take.

Welcome to general conference and to the privilege of hearing the voice of the Lord.

It is my great privilege [to hear] the voice of the Lord in general conference.
